## LAYER 0 — Origins of Surname

The surname **Koch** is German and Jewish (Ashkenazic) in origin, derived from the Middle High German *koch* — meaning simply **"cook."** It is one of the most common German surnames, an occupational name given to those who prepared food professionally, equivalent to the English surname "Cook." The name appears across German-speaking Europe from the medieval period onward and spread globally through Dutch, German, and Ashkenazi Jewish emigration in the 18th and 19th centuries.

The specific Koch line that produced Fred C. Koch and Koch Industries traces to the **Netherlands**, not mainland Germany. The founder's father, **Harry Koch**, was born in **Workum, Friesland, Netherlands** (a northern Dutch province) in approximately 1867 and emigrated to the United States in **1888** — first passing through The Hague before crossing the Atlantic. Harry Koch was a **printer by trade**, trained as a printer's apprentice in Workum, and carried that craft with him to America, eventually settling in **Quanah, Texas**, where he purchased the failing local newspaper, *The Tribune-Chief*, and became its publisher and printer.

Harry Koch married **Mattie B. (née Mixson)**, an American woman, and together they raised their son **Fred Chase Koch** in Quanah. The family was described as "reasonably well off" — well enough for Fred to attend both Rice University and MIT, two of the most expensive and competitive universities in early 20th century America.

**Pre-Founder Generational Lineage:**
1. Dutch printing family in Workum, Friesland, Netherlands — no named ancestors documented prior to Harry
2. **Harry Koch** (born ~1867, Workum, Netherlands; died ~1942) — Dutch immigrant printer, newspaper publisher; arrived USA 1888; settled Quanah, Texas; purchased *The Tribune-Chief* newspaper; married Mattie B. Mixson
3. **Mattie B. (née Mixson) Koch** — American wife of Harry; mother of Fred C. Koch
4. → **FOUNDER: Fred Chase Koch** (1900–1967)

> **Note on Harry Koch:** The grandfather of Charles and David Koch immigrated from the Netherlands in 1888 — a Dutch Protestant working-class printer who became a small-town Texas newspaper publisher. He represents the immigrant-American bootstrap origin that Fred Koch would invoke repeatedly in his political writings. Harry's Dutch origins explain the family's pronunciation — *"KOHK"* not *"KOTCH"* — which mirrors Dutch phonology.

> **Note on the Koch name dispute:** "Koch" appears in multiple, completely unrelated American family lines. The family most relevant to this dossier is entirely distinct from the Volga German Koch line and the various Ashkenazi Koch families. This is strictly the Dutch-Texas-Wichita industrial line.

**Wealth Origin Narrative:** Fred Koch graduated from MIT in 1922, briefly worked for the Texas Company, then founded his own engineering firm in 1925 (initially Winkler-Koch Engineering). In 1927 he developed a significantly improved thermal cracking process for refining heavy crude oil into gasoline — a process that immediately threatened the major oil company monopolies that held most refining patents. The major oil companies sued him repeatedly (over 40 lawsuits filed), tying up the technology in courts for years and forcing him to seek international clients. This led him to build oil refineries for the Soviet Union in the early 1930s — work he later described as personally repellent. By 1940 he co-founded Wood River Oil and Refining Company in East St. Louis. Over the 1940s and 1950s he consolidated his oil refining operations, acquired ranching and pipeline assets, and built a vertically integrated industrial company. He co-founded the John Birch Society in 1958, reflecting a fierce anti-communist, anti-government ideology that he passed directly to sons Charles and David. He died of a heart attack at 67 on a quail-hunting trip — having built a company worth $21 million, which Charles would grow over 60 years to over $100 billion in revenue.

## LAYER 2 — Second Generation: Fred's Four Sons

> The four Koch brothers became one of the most litigated family business disputes in American history. **Frederick and Bill** sued **Charles and David** in 1980 claiming they had been cheated out of shares. The lawsuit lasted over a decade. A settlement was reached in 1983. Bill continued to litigate for years afterward. By the end, Charles and David controlled Koch Industries, while Frederick and Bill were bought out.

## Site Builder Notes

1. **GOLD BORDER — Frederick R. Koch:** The eldest brother is the clearest gold-border candidate. He not only distanced himself from the business but from the political network, preferred anonymity, spent his life in arts and historic preservation entirely apart from his brothers, and rarely saw them in his final years. His entire estate was left to arts promotion rather than family trusts. This is an authentic case for the gold border designation.

2. **EOT ENTRY badge candidates — Critical nodes:**
   - **Fred C. Koch (founder)** — co-founded the John Birch Society (1958); built refineries for Soviet Union and Nazi Germany; foundational figure in American far-right political infrastructure
   - **Charles Koch** — architect of the most powerful dark money political network in American history ($5.3B in stock donated to political vehicles); co-founder Cato Institute; Stand Together/AFP network spent $548M in 2024 alone; documented effort to capture university curriculum via $52.6M in college donations (2022); anti-Trump but anti-labor, anti-regulation throughout career
   - **David Koch** — AFP co-architect; 1980 Libertarian VP candidate; funded climate change denial while Koch Industries was among the largest polluters in the U.S. (documented by EPA and ProPublica)
   - **Koch Network / Stand Together** — the dark money structure itself deserves a standalone evidence item if not already on site

3. **Walton-Koch parallel:** Both families are dominant Industrial/American green-category dynasties, both are from Kansas/Arkansas, both use private holding structures to avoid disclosure, both fund education reform (charter schools), and both have made the largest political donations of any private industrial family in their respective generations.
