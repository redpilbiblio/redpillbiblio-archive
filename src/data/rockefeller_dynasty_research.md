## LAYER 0 — Origins of Surname

The name "Rockefeller" is German in origin — a compound of *Rocken* (rye field or rocky field) and *Feld* (field). It derives from the village of **Rockenfeld**, a now-abandoned settlement in the Rhineland-Palatinate region of western Germany, near the town of Neuwied on the Rhine River. The family can be traced to this area as early as the **early 17th century**, when records show a **Johann Rockefeller** (c.1634–1684) living in the region. Rockenfeld no longer appears on modern maps — it was absorbed into surrounding communities over the centuries — but it gave the family both its name and its earliest documented geographic identity.

The American branch of the family descends from **Johann Peter Rockenfeller** (the original German spelling), who was baptized on **27 September 1682** in the Protestant church of Rengsdorf, near Altwied (today a district of Neuwied). He emigrated from the Rhineland in **1723**, arriving in Pennsylvania (then a British colony) with three children. He was among the large wave of German-speaking "Palatine" emigrants who left the Rhine region in the early 18th century fleeing poverty, war, and religious persecution. Johann Peter settled first in Germantown, Pennsylvania (then a predominantly German-speaking township), before relocating to Somerville and Amwell Township, New Jersey, where he became a plantation owner and landholder — a remarkable leap from Rhineland peasant to American property owner within one generation.

Contemporary records from 1723 immigration lists confirm his arrival. By the late 1700s, the family had intermarried with other Palatine-descent families in New Jersey and New York, creating the hybrid Scots-Irish/German lineage that would produce John D. Rockefeller through his mother Eliza Davison.

**Pre-Founder Generational Lineage:**
1. Johann Rockefeller (c.1634–1684) — Rhineland, earliest documented ancestor; Protestant church records Rengsdorf
2. **Johann Peter Rockenfeller** (bapt. 27 Sep 1682 – d. c.1763) — emigrated to Pennsylvania 1723; plantation owner in New Jersey; *true American progenitor*
3. Peter Rockefeller (1711–c.1787) — New Jersey farmer/landowner
4. William Rockefeller (c.1750–c.1793) — New Jersey
5. William Avery Rockefeller (1810–1906) — traveling medicine man, confidence man, bigamist; father of John D.; nicknamed "Devil Bill"; taught sons "trade sharp" but also gave John D. his entrepreneurial instincts and early capital
6. → **FOUNDER: John Davison Rockefeller Sr.** (1839–1937)

> **Note on William Avery Rockefeller ("Devil Bill"):** The founder's father was a charismatic and amoral traveling salesmen who sold supposed cancer cures and other patent medicines. He was a bigamist — he legally married a second woman (Margaret L. Allen) in 1855 while still married to Eliza Davison — and periodically abandoned the family for months at a time. He lived to age 95 under a false name ("Dr. William Levingston") and is buried in South Dakota. The contrast between "Devil Bill" and his son John D.'s moral rectitude (teetotaler, deeply religious Baptist) is one of history's most striking generational reversals.

**Wealth Origin Narrative:** At age 16, John D. got his first bookkeeping job in Cleveland (1855) and taught himself accounting. In 1859, aged 23, he borrowed $1,000 from his father and invested it with partner Maurice Clark into a produce commission business. That same year, oil was struck at Titusville, Pennsylvania. In 1863, Rockefeller entered oil refining. By 1865, he bought out his partners for $72,500. In 1870, he co-founded Standard Oil with $1 million capital. By 1880, Standard controlled 90% of U.S. refining. His method: vertical integration, railroad rebates (secret deals for lower freight rates unavailable to competitors), and systematic buyout of rivals — offering them Standard Oil stock or bankruptcy. In 1882, Standard Oil Trust was formed — the first major American trust — valued at $70M. In 1890, the Sherman Antitrust Act was passed partly in response to Standard Oil. In 1911, the Supreme Court ordered Standard Oil broken into 34 companies. Rockefeller, still a major shareholder in all of them, became *wealthier* as the breakup unlocked value. He spent the last 40 years of his life giving away money — over $500 million — and lived to 97.

## Notes for Site Builders

1. **"Devil Bill" Rockefeller** is one of the most compelling pre-founder story elements in any American dynasty — a bigamist traveling medicine man whose son became the richest American in history. This should be prominently featured in Layer 0.

2. **Michael Rockefeller (1938–1961?)** — Nelson's son who disappeared in Papua New Guinea during an anthropological expedition. Body never found. This is a genuine unsolved mystery with documented evidence suggesting he may have been killed by Asmat tribespeople. High-value cross-link to your site's existing items if any cover missing persons or intelligence-linked deaths.

3. **EOT ENTRY badge candidates:**
   - **David Rockefeller Sr.** — Trilateral Commission founder (key globalist network node), CFR Chairman, Chase/China banking
   - **Nelson Rockefeller** — VP under Ford (never elected VP or President), "Rockefeller Drug Laws" 1973 (mass incarceration precursor), "Happy Rockefeller affair" scandal
   - **Jay Rockefeller IV** — Senate Intelligence Committee Chairman during major NSA surveillance authorizations

4. **Rockefeller Capital Management privatization note:** The Rockefeller family no longer directly controls Rockefeller Capital Management — it was restructured as a standalone firm in 2018 and is now majority-owned by external investors. David Jr. sits on the board but is NOT the controlling owner. The firm uses the Rockefeller name under license. This is an important distinction vs. Rothschild & Co where family still controls 95%+ of voting rights.

5. **Fossil Fuel Divestment (2014):** The Rockefeller Brothers Fund publicly announced divestment from fossil fuels in September 2014 — notable given that the entire family fortune originated from oil. Cross-link potential with your Environmental Crimes pillar.

6. **Total family wealth ~$10.3 billion (2024)** — spread across 180+ living family members from JDR Sr.'s line. Per-capita wealth is therefore far lower than single-family dynasties like Al Saud or Walton.

7. **"Room 5600"** — the family office at Rockefeller Center, 30 Rockefeller Plaza, 56th Floor. Still exists and manages family investments and trusts for the extended family.
