## LAYER 0 — Origins of Surname

The Walton surname is English in origin, derived from the Old English *Weald-tun* — meaning "farm by a forest" or "settlement near a wood." It appears consistently in English records from the 13th century onward and spread widely through the British Isles before emigrating to colonial America. There is no single ancestral village; the name arose independently in multiple English counties, making it one of Britain's more common topographic surnames.

The American Walton line that produced Sam Walton can be traced to **Scots-Irish and English Protestant settlers** who entered the American colonies via Virginia and the Carolinas in the 1700s and migrated steadily westward across Appalachia, through Kentucky and Tennessee, into Missouri and finally into Oklahoma Territory — following the great arc of American frontier expansion across three centuries.

Sam Walton's branch descends from **mid-19th-century Missouri farming stock.** His grandfather, **Jesse Walton**, was a farmer in Henry County, Missouri. His father, **Thomas Gibson Walton** (1892–1984), was born in Diggins, Webster County, Missouri, and worked variously as a farmer, mortgage broker, banker, and real estate and insurance agent — moving his family frequently through rural Missouri and Oklahoma during Sam's childhood. Thomas lived to age 92, long enough to see his son become the richest man in America.

The family line carries no noble title, no inherited capital, and no dynastic banking connections. What it carried was a particular psychology: frugality, relentless work, salesmanship, and a deeply practical intelligence — values Thomas Gibson Walton explicitly modeled and Sam Walton consciously absorbed, replicated, and institutionalized into the world's largest retail company.

**Pre-Founder Generational Lineage:**
1. English/Scots-Irish colonial settlers (1700s Virginia/Carolina) — no specific named ancestor documented prior to Missouri
2. Jesse Walton — farmer, Henry County, Missouri (mid-1800s)
3. **Thomas Gibson Walton** (1892–1984) — born Diggins, Webster Co., MO; farmer, banker, mortgage man; moved family to Kingfisher, Oklahoma 1917; returned to Missouri 1923; worked as farm loan appraiser and insurance agent through the Depression
4. Nancy Lee Lawrence Walton — Sam's mother; strong character who encouraged Sam's ambition and taught Sunday School
5. → **FOUNDER: Samuel Moore Walton** (1918–1992)

> **Note on Thomas Gibson Walton:** The founder's father was a practical, frugal Depression-era man of many trades who never accumulated significant wealth but modeled the work ethic and dealmaking instincts Sam Walton would later describe as foundational. Sam credited both his mother's drive and his father's trading ability as primary influences. Thomas lived to 92 — long enough to see Walmart become America's largest retailer.

> **Note on Bud Walton:** Sam's younger brother **James Lawrence "Bud" Walton** (1921–1995) was his business partner and co-founder of Walmart. Bud managed store expansion and real estate acquisitions. His descendants (Ann Walton Kroenke and Nancy Walton Laurie) inherited a substantial Walmart stake and are documented in Layer 3 below.

**Wealth Origin Narrative:** Sam Walton graduated from the University of Missouri in 1940 with a business degree, worked briefly for JCPenney ($75/month), then served as an Army intelligence officer during WWII (1942–1945). In 1945, using $5,000 of his own savings and $20,000 borrowed from his father-in-law L.S. Robson, he purchased a Butler Brothers Ben Franklin franchise variety store in Newport, Arkansas. He grew it into the top Ben Franklin store in the region — only to lose the lease in 1950 when his landlord took the store for his own son. This catastrophic setback taught Walton to always negotiate long leases with renewal options — a lesson he applied to every future Walmart store. He relocated to Bentonville, Arkansas, opened a new store, and began acquiring variety stores across the region. In 1962 he opened the first Walmart in Rogers, Arkansas — competing directly against Kmart and Target, which also opened their first stores that same year. His edge: lower prices through direct manufacturer relationships, real estate discipline, and a logistics and distribution system that kept costs radically lower than competitors. Walmart went public in 1970. A $1,650 investment in the IPO was worth $7 million by 1992. By 1991 Walmart surpassed Sears as America's largest retailer. Walton was diagnosed with hairy cell leukemia in 1982, went into remission, then developed bone marrow cancer in 1991. He received the Presidential Medal of Freedom three weeks before his death.

## Notes for Site Builders

1. **EOT ENTRY badge candidates:**
   - **Sam Walton** — Walmart's labor practices, union suppression, and destruction of American small-town retail economies are extensively documented; cross-link to Labor/Economic Crimes pillar if it exists
   - **Rob Walton** — $16.5M in Republican donations 2023–2024 is the single largest political donation by any NFL owner; cross-link to Election Integrity / Dark Money items
   - **Jim Walton** — Arvest Bank is the largest bank in Arkansas with $26B in assets owned by a private family; cross-link to Financial Systems pillar; also charter school PAC donations
   - **Greg Penner** — as Walmart Chairman and Denver Broncos controlling owner, he is the operating nexus of the largest company on earth by revenue

2. **John Walton's death (2005)** — he died piloting a homebuilt experimental ultralight aircraft he personally constructed. The story that Christy Walton was "the world's richest woman" because she inherited his estate was substantially false — Bloomberg's 2015 investigation into court documents revealed most of his $18.2B went to his son Lukas and charitable trusts. This is a documented media misinformation story worth cross-referencing.

3. **Walmart's $681 billion FY2025 revenue** makes it larger than the GDP of most countries on Earth. Context: it employs ~2.1 million Americans (more than the U.S. military), making it the largest private employer in the United States.

4. **Sam's Club** is frequently overlooked as a Walton vehicle — it's a $90B+ revenue membership warehouse chain (separate from Walmart) that operates 600 locations.

5. **Charter School Funding** — The Walton Family Foundation has given over $1.7 billion to charter school expansion since 2000, making them the single largest private funder of charter school expansion in American history. This is a documented and frequently contested policy intervention with major implications for public school systems.

6. **Gold border candidate:** None firmly established — no known Walton family members have publicly denounced the family or changed their name to avoid association.
